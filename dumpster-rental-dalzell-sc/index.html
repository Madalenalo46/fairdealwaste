Frequently Asked Questions

Can I put anything I want in the dumpster?

Almost. You can throw in most common household junk and construction debris. However, we cannot accept hazardous materials like paint, chemicals, oils, asbestos, or flammable liquids.

Can I put the dumpster on the street?

Maybe. It depends on your local city or township. Most public streets require a special permit issued by the municipality. It's your responsibility to secure that permit before we deliver. It's much easier to put it on your own property.

What if the storm knocked down trees and part of my house? Can it all go in one dumpster?

Yes. You can mix organic debris like trees and branches with construction debris like wood, shingles, and siding all in the same roll off dumpster.

How much does waterlogged debris really weigh?

A lot more than you think. A standard sheet of drywall can more than double in weight when soaked. Waterlogged carpet is incredibly heavy. This is why it’s important to get the right size dumpster; the weight can add up fast. We’ll help you choose the right can for the job.

What’s the most common mistake your customers make?

Trying to save a hundred bucks by ordering a dumpster that's too small. It's a classic blunder. You end up needing a second dumpster, which costs you way more in the long run. If you're not sure, go with the bigger size.

How do I pay for the rental?

We typically take payment via credit card when you place your order. This streamlines the process so you don't have to worry about being there with a check for delivery or getting a bill in the mail later. It’s all handled upfront.
